Entering the State House, etiquette, etc

What to wear: Anything you like, but you cannot wear any political messaging, buttons, patches or bring flags or signs into the State House. This includes messages on shirts/clothes.

Remember: You are in the State House not only representing yourself, but an entire movement. Act accordingly. We are dealing with folks who already don’t see us as human. This is not the place to give them more ammo. It isn’t fair but it’s the truth.

Entering/security: You will go through security/metal detector. No weapons are allowed. If they determine something could be a weapon you usually are given the chance to take it outside/store it in your vehicle. This is why arriving early is important, so you have time to potentially run to your car and back.

The committee meeting is in Room 206. (The statehouse entrance is on the 2nd floor. To get to the room, enter the statehouse, go through security and immediately turn right and go down the hall. Do not get on the elevators.)

If we do have enough people to pack out room 206, we will be packed into the hallway outside like sardines. State House employees whose job it is to keep the hallways accessible will tell you to stand to one side, may ask you to move if you’re blocking a doorway, etc. Please listen to them and be polite. They are genuinely nice folks who do not get paid enough.

Once the meeting begins

  • Silence your phones. The chair will likely make an announcement to this effect as well.
  • Committee Chairs are basically God in the room. Whatever they say goes. Do NOT argue back. Polite, deferential communication will take you far. Treat the chair like a judge and the committee room like a courtroom.
  • No clapping, cheering, etc. Treat the committee room like a courtroom.
  • You can record on your phone, just make sure it doesn’t make any noise.
