ALTRAC is just one of many organizations in Alabama working to advance progressive values, stop bad legislation from passing, and get resources in the hands of trans folks that need them. If you are interested in keeping up to date on other legislation, events, resources, and news from throughout the state, please check out these organizations:

  • The Campaign for Southern Equality is working to build a South where LGBTQ+ people are equal in every part of life. In particular, their Trans Youth Emergency Project can help trans youth affected by gender-affirming care restrictions get access to that care.
  • The Alabama's Transgender and Gender Diversity Organizations (ALTGO) website can help you connect with online and in-person transgender support groups, find trans-friendly healthcare providers, get information on updating your legal documentation, and more.
  • The Alabama ACLU is our local chapter of the American Civil Liberties Union. They work on issues around free speech, discrimination based on protected classes, and police misconduct.
  • United Women of Color, based in Madison County, is working to create an inclusive community by building relationships and facilitating authentic connections to address the community's changing needs.
  • The League of Women Voters of Alabama is working to protect and advance voting rights in our state.
  • Alabama Arise is a statewide, member-led organization advancing public policies to improve the lives of Alabamians who are marginalized by poverty.
  • Alabama Values Progress is a media advocacy organization providing frequent updates on what's happening in the legislative session.
  • TAKE Birmingham is a group focusing on breaking down barriers and setting standards within the Birmingham community for trans-related issues, like workplace discrimination, housing advocacy, and more!
  • The Knights & Orchids Society is a direct services organization to help more Black trans, same-gender-loving, and Black queer folks access high-quality primary care and endocrinology services.
  • URGE is a reproductive justice organization driven by and for young people. They are creating a world where all can live with the justice, freedom, safety, and joy we deserve.
